RS232
Found in CNCs, lab instrument scales
  • Point-to-point only
  • Short range (<15 m recommended)
  • Baud rates: 9600, 19200, 115200
  • D-sub connector (DB9 most common)
  • 9 pins in two rows (5 + 4)
  • Labeled COM, RS232, or Serial
RS485
Multi-drop networks used in Modbus RTU
  • Multi-point: up to 32 devices (247 w/ repeaters)
  • Long range up to 1200 m
  • Differential signaling ‚Äî noise resistant
  • Screw terminal blocks (2‚Äì3 wires)
  • Labeled A/B, +/‚Äì, D+/D‚Äì, or D0/D1
  • Sometimes RJ45 on industrial units
RJ45 (Ethernet)
Ethernet, Modbus TCP, PROFINET
  • 10 / 100 / 1000 Mbps (Gigabit common)
  • Up to 100 m per cable segment
  • Industrial variants are ruggedized / lockable
  • 8-pin modular jack (larger phone jack)
  • LED indicator for link / activity
  • Labeled ETH, LAN, or Ethernet symbol
M12
IP67 dust & water proof found in Sensors & Ethernet in harsh environments
  • Vibration-resistant screw lock
  • Standard in robotics & outdoor automation
  • 4, 5, or 8-pin configurations
  • Circular threaded connector (12 mm dia.)
  • 1, 3, 4, 5, or 12 pins
  • A-code (sensors), D-code (Ethernet), X-code (10G)
USB
Programming, HMI configuration
  • USB 2.0: 480 Mbps | USB 3.0: up to 10 Gbps
  • Can supply power (5 V, up to 100 W USB PD)
  • Cable limit ~5 m without active extension
  • Type A (rectangular / host side)
  • Type B: square (beveled / device side)
  • Mini / Micro / USB-C for compact devices

  • Labeled GPIB or IEEE-488
